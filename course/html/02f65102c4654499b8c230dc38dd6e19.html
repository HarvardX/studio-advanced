Behold the grail.

Adaptive learning is what we've all been promised since the first person involved in online education wrote a grant application. Unfortunately, it turns out to be really difficult. So we're not going to give you "the solution" here, because A: there's no one solution, and B: there's no magic wand that will "adaptivize" your course. Instead, we'll show a few options, talk about the work you'll need to do regardless of what option you choose, and give you a realistic view of the field.

The goal: Learn about options for adaptivity in edX.
Prerequisites: To learn about it: you only need to know what an LTI tool is. To actually implement it: everything. You need HTML, CSS, Javscript, design skills, Python or another server-side language, experience running a server, and a good injection of extra cash.
Project length: Be ready to spend a year.
Ingredients:

  • Lots of variants on (or extensions of) your course materials.
  • Other than that, it depends on what you want to do. Sit tight and we'll give you some links.
