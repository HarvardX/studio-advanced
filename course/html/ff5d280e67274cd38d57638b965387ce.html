Examples of Alternative Navigation

Some edX courses have already implemented forms of alternative or augmented navigation. Here are a few that you might want to peruse while considering your own approach.

  • The first run of The Quantum World, from HarvardX, has a page known as "The Tunneler" (it's a quantum physics joke). It has a separate list of course pages, filterable by topic or content. The code that created this can be found at the HX-XML repository. The nice part is that you don't have to enter all the links by hand; the down side is that you need to basically have the course complete before you create this or you'll have to touch it up later.
  • PredictionX: John Snow and the Cholera Epidemic of 1854, also from HarvardX, uses a version of our image carousel to let learners jump between the most important pages in the course.
  • Fantastic Places, Unhuman Humans: Exploring Humanity Through Literature, from Brown University, has a "course map" that's literally a map - which fits their topic quite well.
