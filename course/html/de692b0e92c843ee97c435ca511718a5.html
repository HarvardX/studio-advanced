In December 2018 edX instituted a new policy called Feature-Based Enrollment (FBE). In layman's terms, it's a paywall. In this section we'll talk about what's behind the paywall, what's not, and how you can build your course so that it fits within these restrictions.

The goal: Explore the strategies for making the most of the edX paywall.
Prerequisites: None.
Project length: 30 minutes to learn, 20 minutes to implement, 10 minutes to test.
Ingredients: n/a

Why a paywall?

As a non-profit corporation, edX has always been supported by donations and partner fees (which vary depending on the institution). Verified Certificates provide another source of revenue.

FBE was aimed at improving the financial sustainability of the edX platform by increasing the percentage of people who buy certificates. In other words, it helps edX make enough money to stay in business. While edX is still a non-profit organization that provides open-source code, this money pays for dozens of programmers, support staff, and administrative staff.

EdX did consider other approaches to financial sustainability, such as puting the discussion forums behind a paywall (which they rejected). FBE was considered the best balance between "upsell" and free education. They continue to consider further options.
