Example: Climate Change and Health Outcomes

In HarvardX's course The Health Effects of Climate Change, much of the course revolves around complex interrelationships between a large number of factors in climate change research and human health outcomes. Some of this information is captured in the graphic below:

Even here, however, things have been substantially simplified. The graphic doesn't show the dense spiderweb of connections, only shows cause and effect as general left-to-right-trend, and omits the populations most affected by various changes in our environment. Accessibility is also a major concern, as concisely describing this network for the visually impaired is an exercise in futility.
