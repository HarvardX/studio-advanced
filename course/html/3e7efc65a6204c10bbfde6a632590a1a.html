Alternative Uses

The code we provided will pop up arbitrary HTML. What else can you do with that? Well, it's a little bit limited by the amount of time you have. The links are only present for five seconds. However, in five seconds you can still...

  • Show a simple graph illustrating the professor's comments.
  • Show a locator map to indicate a country or province.
  • When the professor refers to an equation, show that equation for a moment. MathJax works just fine in these links.

All of this could be done in video edits as well, but the advantage here is that you can also make it a link! We encourage you to be consistent throughout your videos. For instance, if you're using it for graphs, use the same type of graph over and over again. Popping up a new type of graph might not give learners enough time to absorb it in five seconds. They can always pause the video, but not everyone thinks of that at the time.

In addition, if you love bad ideas, you could...

  • Block the whole video with a huge opaque black image.
  • Dig up facepalm.gif to add snarky commentary about your videos.
  • Pop up another, smaller iframed video!

Repurposing the Code

HX-JS is provided under the MIT License, which means you can repurpose the code for all sorts of other things. HarvardX has used it for...

  • Popping up whole problems instead of links.
  • Playing a chime when the video reaches a certain time, to alert users that something is coming up or to provide extra information for blind users.
  • Determining the amount of time for which a learner viewed a video (or at least had the video playing in the background while they made a sandwich).

If you're a programmer, you are more than welcome to use our code as a base to create your own video-based tools for edX.
