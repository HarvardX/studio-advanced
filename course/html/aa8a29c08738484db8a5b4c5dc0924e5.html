Question: How do you know what worked?

Short version: Educational research is an entire field of study and we're not writing you a one-pager on it.

Long version: Regardless of what you're testing, you will need to decide what your succeses measures are before you implement. A few questions to consider:

  • Are you measuring learner performance on tests? Is "performance" just their grade? Does it include speed of performance? Number of attempts? Persistence/retention? Long-term recall?
  • Are you measuring survey responses?
  • Are your tests and surveys validated to ensure that they measure what you think they measure?
  • Do you have a baseline measurement for your learners' content knowledge or affective state from earlier in the course?

If item validity and baseline measurements are unfamiliar to you, contact your local educational researcher and ask for help. "We'll figure it out later" is not how good research gets done. Planning is key. Testing content without a plan for evaluating its success is just a waste of time.
