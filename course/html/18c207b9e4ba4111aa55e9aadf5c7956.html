Clickable Image Maps


Easy

Hour

HTML

Labeled diagrams are neat, but sometimes you want more than a single line of text. Maybe you've got a diagram of the cell and you want people to find out more about the parts, or an engineering diagram that explains why things were built the way they were. That's when you want a clickable image with pop-ups.

Clickable images are great for complex diagrams, but they have some of the same accessibility challenges. Your learners will still be able to get to the pop-ups with this approach, but your text will need to explain where in the diagram the object is.

Prerequisites: Familiarity with HTML.
Project length: 10 minutes to learn, 20 minutes to implement, 5 minutes to test.
Ingredients:

  • hx.js and related files, which you can obtain from the HX-JS repository (follow the implementation instructions there). Download them and put them in Files & Uploads.
  • An image that you provide yourself.
  • An HTML image map, which you can create using graphics software or at sites like image-maps.com/.
