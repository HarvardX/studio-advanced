Random problem ordering

We've mentioned elsewhere how Content Experiments are great for research, but you can also use them just to provide problems in a random manner! Create some extra assignments, create your Content Experiment groups, and move things around so that different learners see slightly different questions.

You can combine this with the randomization above, but this approach really shines when you have problems that are difficult to randomize. Things like Open Response Assessments, drag-and-drop questions, questions you've brought in via LTI, all those complex things that are cool and unique, you can still randomize those! You just need to create a few spares.

Another option is a content library, which we've discussed in detail elsewhere in the course. We just wanted to give you a little reminder here: you can pull just a handful of problems from a larger library, so that most people will be seeing different sets of problems. Follow that link to get more info.
