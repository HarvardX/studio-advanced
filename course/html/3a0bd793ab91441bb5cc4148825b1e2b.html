Encouraging Verification

What can you do if you want to encourage your learners to purchase a verified certificate? This is aside from discussions of price points, willingness to pay, etc. We nerds writing this course don't know anything about that anyway.

💰 Give Free Samples

Don't wall off everything. That's like putting together a portfolio and leaving out your best work. No one's going to give you money based on that. You need to give people a taste of what the course is going to be like.

We recommend having at least a quarter of your assessments visible to all learners. Make these real assessments, not just the easy stuff. That way your learners will know what they're signing up for when they go to buy a verified certificate.

😨 Build Cliffhangers

Your learners will realize that there are some assessments they don't see, just from the presence of the lock icon. However, they might not see them as very important. If you want to entice people to pay for a verified certificate, you need to make them a crucial, integral part of the learning experience.

One way to do that is to create intentional, obvious lead-ins to the assignments. Give your learners the beginning of a story in a place that everyone can see, and the resolution to that story in a gated assignment. Show the materials for an intriguing assignment in HTML that everyone can access, but keep the assignment itself behind the paywall. Make a game out of it, then make them want to win the game.
